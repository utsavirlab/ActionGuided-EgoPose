Action-Guided Egocentric 3D Pose Estimation

Sadia Mubashshira, Nazanin F. Karimi, Kevin Desai

University of Texas at San Antonio

Paper arXiv Code

Abstract

We propose an action-guided framework for egocentric 3D human pose estimation that integrates spatial appearance cues and long-term motion dynamics. Our approach addresses severe occlusion and limited field-of-view challenges by leveraging action-aware temporal reasoning.

Key Contributions

  • Dual-stream spatial and temporal modeling for egocentric pose.
  • Action-guided transformer fusion with learnable joint tokens.
  • Graph-based reasoning and LSTM for temporally coherent 3D predictions.

Method

1. Spatial Feature Extraction

  • ResNet backbone extracts high-level semantic features.
  • Encodes static scene context for occluded joints.

2. Temporal Motion Modeling

  • Captures long-term motion dynamics across frames.
  • Ensures temporal consistency.

3. Action-Guided Fusion

  • Transformer decoder integrates spatial and motion streams.
  • Joint-wise adaptive fusion.

4. 3D Pose Regression

  • Graph reasoning for inter-joint constraints.
  • LSTM for sequential modeling.
