AG-EgoPose: Leveraging Action-Guided Motion and Kinematic Joint Encoding for Egocentric 3D Pose Estimation

Md Mushfiqur Azam, John Quarles, Kevin Desai
The University of Texas at San Antonio (UTSA), San Antonio, TX, USA
Preprint
Paper Code

Abstract

Egocentric 3D human pose estimation remains challenging due to severe perspective distortion, limited body visibility, and complex camera motion in first-person views. AG-EgoPose is a dual-stream framework that integrates short- and long-range motion context with fine-grained spatial cues. A spatial stream predicts 2D joint heatmaps and embeds them into joint-specific tokens, while a temporal stream extracts action-guided motion features. These representations are fused with a transformer decoder using learnable joint tokens to regress 3D joint coordinates. Experiments on EgoPW and SceneEgo show improved quantitative and qualitative performance.

Method Overview

AG-EgoPose follows a dual-stream architecture. The spatial stream extracts joint-aware heatmap embeddings from egocentric frames, while the temporal stream captures long-term motion dynamics using ActionFormer. These features are fused using a transformer decoder with learnable joint tokens to regress 3D joint coordinates.

Spatial Heatmap Module

The spatial stream employs an encoder–decoder heatmap network with unified skip connections. Multi-scale feature fusion improves joint localization robustness under fisheye distortion.

Qualitative Results

Qualitative comparison on SceneEgo and EgoPW. AG-EgoPose produces more stable and anatomically consistent poses under egocentric distortion and partial visibility.
